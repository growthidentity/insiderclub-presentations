What your tap water really contains

01

Microplastics in Drinking Water

Plastic bottles and packaging break down into microplastics that eventually end up in our drinking water. Standard filters often can't catch them.

2025 research has found microplastics in human blood, brain, and placenta. These particles cross the body's barriers and accumulate in organs.

Humans ingest up to 52,000 microplastic particles annually. Studies link their accumulation to higher risks of heart disease and neurodegenerative disorders. (Newcastle University, PMC 2025)

02

PFAS - Forever Chemicals

PFAS compounds are called "forever chemicals" because they never break down in nature. They're used in non-stick pans, waterproof fabrics, and firefighting foam.

These chemicals are linked to cancer risk, hormonal disruption, and weakened immune systems.

PFAS compounds accumulate in the body over years. Studies link them to thyroid disease, cancer, and reduced fertility. New EU limits took effect January 2026. (EEA 2024)

03

Pharmaceutical Residues in Water

Antibiotics, hormones, painkillers - medications that people consume partially return to the water cycle. Standard treatment plants don't fully remove them.

Over 100 pharmaceutical substances detected in European waters. Long-term low-dose exposure may disrupt hormones and contribute to antibiotic resistance. (EEA 2024, WHO)

04

Heavy Metals from Old Pipes

Many older buildings still have aging plumbing systems. Old lead and copper pipes release heavy metals into the water, especially when water sits in the pipes overnight.

Lead is particularly dangerous for children - it affects brain development and can cause permanent damage even in small amounts.

Millions of homes worldwide still use pipes that can leach heavy metals.
